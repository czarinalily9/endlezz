First-Time Model, First-Time Cropped Polo

Growing up, I never imagined myself in front of the camera. I was always more of an observer—admiring how style could transform someone, how a well-put-together outfit could change the way a person carried themselves. But when the opportunity came for me to model for the first time, I found myself embracing something I had loved all along: button-downs and polos.

A Wardrobe with a Story

My love for polos isn’t just about fashion—it’s about legacy. My dad had a collection of button-downs and polos that felt like an extension of who he was. Clean, timeless, and effortlessly cool. I remember watching him carefully choose which one to wear, appreciating the way each piece carried its own personality.

As I grew older, I started borrowing them. At first, it was just out of curiosity—testing how they fit, rolling up the sleeves, pairing them with different pieces. But over time, they became a part of my own identity. Eventually, I inherited almost his entire wardrobe of polos, and with that came a connection to something greater than just clothing.

The First Step into Modeling—And a First-Time Cropped Polo

When I stepped in front of the camera for the first time, wearing a button-down that once belonged to my dad, I felt a mix of nervousness and confidence. The nerves came from doing something unfamiliar—standing under bright lights, posing, and learning to move with ease. But the confidence? That came from the shirt I was wearing.

Except this time, it wasn’t just any polo—it was a cropped one.

I had never worn a cropped polo before, and it felt like stepping into completely new territory. I was so used to oversized button-downs that the fitted, structured yet playful silhouette was a challenge. But as soon as I saw myself in the mirror, I realized something: it still felt like me. The essence of what I loved—the clean lines, the crisp collar, the effortless vibe—was all there. Just in a fresh, unexpected way.

Why Polos and Button-Downs?

For me, polos and button-downs are more than just classic staples—they’re a representation of my journey. They remind me of my dad’s quiet elegance, of the first time I experimented with style, and of the moment I took my first step into modeling.

Whether dressed up or down, oversized or cropped, polos will always be my go-to. They’re versatile, effortless, and most importantly, they tell a story—my story.
